Muscatine's Beginning

Blackhawk War April–August 1832

The Black Hawk War was a conflict between the United States and Native Americans led by Black Hawk, a Sauk leader. Following his defeat, Black Hawk and his allies were forced to cede a 50-mile-wide strip of land along the western banks of the Mississippi River, officially known as the Black Hawk Purchase. This would include the land that became Muscatine.

First Settlers 1833

Muscatine, seat of Muscatine County, first came into existence in the summer of 1833 when Colonel George Davenport of Rock Island, Illinois, sent three representatives into the territory to set up a trading post. The outpost was nicknamed "Casey's Woodpile" OR MAYBE "Casey's Wood Yard of Newburg" because the primary business of the depot was supplying the passing steamboats with cut wood to burn for fuel.

Bloomington to Muscatine June 7, 1849

The town referred to as Bloomington was originally incorporated in 1839, but the name led to confusion. The town's citizens had petitioned for the change because mail was frequently being misrouted to other towns named Bloomington in neighboring states.

1840s–1890s

Lumber Town

The lumber industry boomed in Muscatine, Iowa, due to its strategic location on the Mississippi River, which allowed for cheap transport of logs from northern forests.

THIS IS FROM https://www.muscatineiowa.gov/341/Muscatine-HistoryLumbering, saw and planing mills, sash and door factories, and other forms of woodworking were a major industry in Muscatine. When the nearby forests were cut off, lumber operators moved up the Mississippi River and brought the logs downstream by water. With the coming of the railroad, lumber was shipped in, but through all these changes various types of woodworking continued to be Muscatine's major industry. Muscatine businessmen acquired forest lands and continued to operate saw mills in various parts of the county.

1854

Mark Twain Works in Muscatine

Mark Twain, called Muscatine home back in 1854. Twain's brother, Orion, was part owner of the still operating Muscatine Journal.

In his book, “Life on the Mississippi”, he fondly recalled the sunsets in Muscatine, writing “I remember Muscatine — still more pleasantly — for its summer sunsets. I have never seen any, on either side of the ocean, that equaled them."

1868

Alexander Clark Wins Desegregation

Alexander G. Clark was a Black laborer, barber, lawyer, diplomat and activist. In 1867, when the Muscatine school district said Clark's daughter, Susan, 12, couldn't attend the same public school that white students attended, he sued. The Iowa Supreme Court ruled in his favor in 1868, saying that all children could attend a common school.

1920s–Today

FRUIT (AND VEGGIE) LAND

Muscatine's famous melon industry — as well as other truck produce — blossomed in the late 19th century. The region's sandy, well-drained soil and micro-climate made it a perfect agricultural hub. It became a major economic force, with hundreds of train carloads of melons shipped across the Midwest annually.

late 1890s–1940s

PEARL CITY

Button factories expanded along the city's industrial edge.

German immigrant John Frederick Boepple founded the first button-cutting factory in Muscatine, utilizing the abundant freshwater mussel shells of the Mississippi River.

THIS IS FROM https://www.muscatineiowa.gov/341/Muscatine-HistoryJohn Fred Boepple, a German immigrant with a flair for making buttons, came to Muscatine in 1887. By mistake, Boepple found that freshwater mussel shells, instead of animal horns, made a stronger button that more closely resembled the fashionable buttons made of expensive import marine shells. Nicholas Barry and his sons invented a machine which turned out better buttons with less labor. Muscatine soon became the world's largest pearl button manufacturer, its factories employing half of the Muscatine workforce during the turn of the century, earning it the name of Pearl Button Capital of the World. In 1905, more than 1.5 billion pearl buttons were made in Muscatine. Today, nearly all buttons are made from plastic.

1890s–Today

Industrialization & Modern Industry

Heinz 1893

Established in 1893, the Heinz plant in Muscatine was the company's first major expansion outside of Pittsburgh. It is the largest Heinz facility outside of corporate headquarters, anchored by a rich history of tomato farming and ongoing, multi-million-dollar modernizations.

Stanley Corporations 1913

Stanley Consultants (1913), HNI Corporation (1947), Stanley Center for Peace and Security (1956).

Kent Worldwide 1936

The growing feed business moves to Muscatine, Iowa, capitalizing on the transportation benefits of the Mississippi River.

Carver Pump 1938

Carver Pump was founded by Roy Carver Sr. in 1938. The original factory was located next to the Mississippi river in Muscatine, Iowa which is about 25 miles southwest of the Quad Cities area.

Grain Processing Corporation 1943

Grain Processing Corporation (GPC) was founded and incorporated on February 11, 1943 in Muscatine, Iowa. It was originally established by S.G. Stein and G.A. Kent to build and operate an industrial alcohol plant for the World War II synthetic rubber program.

Bandag 1957

Bandag was founded in Muscatine in 1957 by American entrepreneur Roy J. Carver. Carver purchased the U.S. rights to a German "cold-cure" tire-retreading process and started operations in a former sauerkraut plant in the city.
